Ford F-150® FAQs


Does the F-150® have a heads-up display?

The 2024 Ford F-150® will have a heads-up display as an option for the LARIAT® on its High (502A) Equipment package, an option with the Tremor® on its High (402A) Equipment package, and standard on King Ranch®, Platinum®, and Raptor®.


What is the Ford F-150® Pro-Access Tailgate?

The 2024 Ford F-150® Pro-Access Tailgate is an innovative addition to the F-150 ® family. Simply said, it is a regular tailgate that contains a door, allowing items in the bed to be retrieved a little easier. Even when hooked to a trailer, the driver-side approach swing-out door allows easy access to the bed of the truck.


Can the Ford F-150® pull a gooseneck trailer?

The 2024 Ford F-150®s can be equipped to tow travel trailers, campers, or goosenecks. The 3.50liter EcoBoost V-6 engine combined with the Max Trailer Tow Package equipped can tow a maximum of 14,000 pounds with the proper configuration. Of course, the maximum amount of weight varies based on cargo, vehicle configureation, accessories and the number of passengers.


Where is the F-150® assembled?

The ford F-150® is assembled in two places: Dearborn, Michigan, which is the home of the Ford Motor Company, and Claycomo, Missouri. They are made for Americans by Americans. For those interested in finding out which plant produced their F-150®, check the 11th digit of the VIN. If there is an F then it was built in Dearborn, MI, and if it is a K then it was built at the Claucomo, MO facility.


Which F-150® trims have a hybrid option?

The 430 horsepower / 570 lb.-ft. of torque* 3.5L PowerBoost™ Full Hybrid V6 engine is available on F-150® XLT, LARIAT®, King Ranch® and Platinum models. Plus, Pro Power Onboard comes standard on all hybrid models.

*Calculated via combined performance of the engine and electric motor(s) with peak battery power. The calculations utilize SAE J1349® engine results and Ford electric motor dyno testing. Your results may vary.
